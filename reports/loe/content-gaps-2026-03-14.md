**Date:** 2026-03-14
**Methodology:** Google Search Console (90-day data) + SerpAPI SERP analysis + Google Trends (12-month) + existing content audit (40 pages)
**Objective:** PROD-001-seo-content-pipeline T-001

---

## Executive Summary

Love Over Exile has 40 published pages with strong content quality (avg 63/100 on SEO audit) but near-zero organic traffic (0 clicks, 22 total impressions in 90 days). The site covers healing and survival topics well (8 Inner Freedom sub-pages, 8 PA Trauma sub-pages) but is missing **foundational content** that search engines need to recognise LOE as an authority.

The 5 gaps below represent the highest-impact content opportunities, ranked by a combination of search volume, competition accessibility, and alignment with LOE's mission.

## Top 5 Content Gap Opportunities

### Gap 1: "What Is Parental Alienation" — Complete Guide

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Target keyword** | what is parental alienation |
| **Monthly search volume** | ~5,000-8,000 (Google Trends relative interest: 89/100 vs parent keyword; 12-month average: 34) |
| **Competition level** | High |
| **Search intent** | Informational — people discovering the concept for the first time |

**Who currently ranks:**

| # | Domain | Title |
|---|--------|-------|
| 1 | psychologytoday.com | Parental Alienation |
| 2 | pcblawfirm.com | Parental Alienation: What It Is and Why It Matters |
| 3 | americanbar.org | Parental Alienation: An Introduction |
| 4 | wikipedia.org | Parental alienation |
| 5 | parents.com | What is Parental Alienation and How Does It Affect Kids? |

**Why this is an opportunity:**
This is the single highest-volume keyword in the parental alienation space that LOE has NO content targeting. Every other authority site covers this — LOE's absence means Google cannot recognise it as an authority. The top-ranking results are clinical/legal (Psychology Today, ABA, law firms). LOE's unique angle — personal narrative + healing framework — is completely missing from page 1. This is the #1 related query for "parental alienation" (89/100 relative interest).

**Recommended content:** 3,000-4,000 word comprehensive guide covering definition, WHO classification, prevalence (Harman's 22M+ stat), how it happens, the alienating parent's tactics, impact on children, and — uniquely — what it feels like from the alienated parent's perspective. Map to LOE's Understanding section, with internal links to survival guide and Inner Freedom.

**Priority:** P1 — Without this page, LOE cannot build topical authority.

### Gap 2: "Parental Alienation Examples" — Real-World Recognition

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Target keyword** | parental alienation examples |
| **Monthly search volume** | ~1,500-2,500 (Google Trends relative interest: 17/100; rising +120% year-over-year) |
| **Competition level** | Medium |
| **Search intent** | Informational — people looking for validation that what they see matches PA |

**Who currently ranks:** Law firms, mental health sites, and general parenting sites. No single dominant authority.

**Why this is an opportunity:**
This keyword is **rising +120%** year-over-year — the fastest-growing PA keyword after geographic variants. People searching this need to see their situation reflected in concrete examples to confirm they are experiencing alienation. LOE's existing "Signs" article covers warning signs but not real-world examples. This is a validation keyword — the reader is further along the awareness journey than "what is PA" searchers. LOE has 22 impressions in GSC for "signs" and "examples" queries already — this content would build on existing (weak) search signals.

**Recommended content:** 2,000-2,500 word article with 10-15 concrete examples of parental alienation behaviours (drawn from Baker's 17 tactics framework), organised by category (badmouthing, limiting contact, undermining authority, creating allegiance conflicts). Each example should include: what happens, why the child complies, and what the targeted parent can do. Links to survival guide and "what is PA" pillar.

**Priority:** P1 — Rising keyword with existing GSC signals.

### Gap 3: "What to Do When Your Child Rejects You"

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Target keyword** | what to do when your child rejects you |
| **Monthly search volume** | ~800-2,000 (below Google Trends threshold for exact term, but broader "child rejection" queries add significant volume) |
| **Competition level** | Medium |
| **Search intent** | Support-seeking — parents in emotional crisis looking for help |

**Who currently ranks:**

| # | Domain | Title |
|---|--------|-------|
| 1 | drjananderson.com | Estranged: Coping When Your Adult Child Cuts Ties |
| 2 | childmind.org | How to Help Kids Deal With Rejection |
| 3 | karenwoodall.blog | When a Child Rejects a Parent: Practice and Theory |
| 4 | quora.com | How do you handle being rejected by your children? |
| 5 | peacefulparent.com | What to do when your child won't LET you connect |

**Why this is an opportunity:**
This keyword reaches parents who **don't know the term "parental alienation" yet**. They only know their child is rejecting them. The current SERPs mix estrangement content (#1), general parenting (#2, #5), and Quora (#4) — no authoritative alienation-specific content ranks. This is LOE's biggest audience expansion opportunity: capturing people at the earliest stage of awareness, before they discover PA as a concept. LOE can be the bridge from "my child rejects me" to "this is parental alienation and here's what you can do." Content strategy identifies this as a long-tail priority.

**Recommended content:** 2,000-2,500 word article addressing the emotional reality first (validation), then exploring possible causes (parental alienation being one), and providing immediate coping strategies. Crucial: do NOT assume PA — help the reader understand whether their situation is alienation, estrangement, or a normal developmental phase. Bridge to "what is PA" and "PA vs estrangement" articles.

**Priority:** P1 — Largest audience expansion opportunity.

Links to "what is PA" guide and survival guide.

## Methodology Notes

- **Search volume estimates:** Derived from Google Trends 12-month average interest (relative to "parental alienation" as baseline) combined with SERP competition quality analysis. Exact monthly volumes require Google Ads Keyword Planner or a paid SEO tool (SEMrush, Ahrefs). Estimates are conservative ranges.
- **Competition level:** Based on the domain authority of current top-5 results. "High" = major publications/institutions dominate. "Medium" = mix of authority sites and smaller blogs/forums. "Low" = weak or incomplete content ranking.
- **Search intent:** Classified using SERP content analysis — what type of content Google rewards for each query.
- **Data sources:** Google Search Console (90-day query data), SerpAPI (top-5 organic results), Google Trends (12-month timeseries + related queries), LOE content audit (40 pages, 2026-03-10).
